Week 4 – Shaping Techniques

After cutting, students bend and shape their metal pieces. Cold forming with a bench vice and hammer or using simple jigs is the primary method. Mark where the bend should start, clamp the metal in the vice and apply even pressure or gentle hammer blows. Bending gradually helps achieve a neat angle. For curves, wrap the metal around a cylindrical former or use a jig. Mild steel is malleable but will spring back slightly, so it may need over‑bending. Heat bending (with a torch) is rarely used in Year 8 but may be demonstrated by the teacher. Always be aware of pinch points and sharp edges during shaping.

Case Study

Maria tries to bend a 5 mm steel rod into a loop using only her hands and finds it nearly impossible. With the teacher’s help she sets up a bending jig and uses a long pipe as a lever to bend the rod gradually around a circular form. The result is a smooth loop. Jake hammers a flat bar in a vice without marking the bend location and ends up with the angle in the wrong spot, making one side too short. He learns to mark the bend line before shaping.
